Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to common questions

The complete process from initial consultation to visa approval typically takes 4-6 months. However, this can vary based on your chosen destination, program start date, and individual circumstances. We recommend starting at least 8-12 months before your intended start date.

Our fees vary depending on the destination and level of support you need. We offer flexible packages from basic application assistance to comprehensive end-to-end support. Contact us for a personalized quote based on your specific requirements. Initial consultation is always free!

Most English-speaking countries require proof of English proficiency through IELTS, TOEFL, or PTE. However, some universities offer waivers if you've completed previous education in English. Germany and some European programs may accept different language tests. We can help determine your specific requirements.

Yes! Most countries allow international students to work part-time during their studies (typically 20 hours per week during term time). Work rights vary by country - Canada, UK, Ireland, and Australia offer the most flexible work opportunities. Some countries also allow full-time work during holidays.

We maintain a 98% visa success rate across all destinations. Our experienced team ensures every application is thoroughly prepared with complete documentation and proper guidance for visa interviews. We stay updated on the latest visa requirements and changes.

Absolutely! Our post-arrival support includes helping you find suitable accommodation, whether on-campus residence or off-campus housing. We provide guidance on neighborhoods, budgeting, and connecting with other students. We also offer pre-departure orientation sessions.

Typical requirements include: academic transcripts, English proficiency test scores, statement of purpose, letters of recommendation (2-3), CV/resume, passport copy, and financial documents showing ability to fund your studies. Specific requirements vary by country and institution - we'll provide a detailed checklist.

Many countries allow you to bring dependents (spouse and children) on a dependent visa. Requirements and work rights for dependents vary by country. Canada, UK, and Australia are particularly family-friendly. We can advise you on the best options for your family situation.

Scholarships range from government-funded (Chevening, DAAD, Fulbright) to university-specific merit awards and external funding. Amounts can cover partial to full tuition, and sometimes living expenses. We help identify opportunities based on your profile and guide you through applications.

We recommend starting 12-18 months before your intended intake. This gives ample time for test preparation, university research, application crafting, scholarship applications, and visa processing. Most universities have deadlines 6-9 months before the start date.
